# Deep Dive 05 — Energy & Power Systems (2026–2036)
*Wave-1 research agent output, full version. Two WebFetch calls hit 403 (Utility Dive paywall) but the underlying figures (GE Vernova ~80 GW backlog into 2029, Mitsubishi capacity-doubling) were corroborated via GE Vernova's own SEC 8-Ks.*

## Core thesis
Power is THE binding constraint on AI and electrification. The investable scarcities are NOT primarily in generation technology — they are in the *physical equipment supply chain, fuel cycle, and skilled labor* that any new generation source must pass through. Deepest moats: large power transformers, gas turbine slots, HALEU enrichment, HVDC cable, journeyman electricians. These bind first (2026–2030), have the longest lead times (3–8 yrs), and are hardest to substitute. SMRs/fusion/EGS mostly arrive AFTER the bottleneck window — optionality, not relief for the acute 2026–2029 squeeze.

## 1. 10-Year roadmap (dated)
- **2026:** US DC demand ~325–400 TWh; 30–50% of planned 2026 US DC builds at risk of delay/cancellation. Palisades restart early 2026. Fervo Cape Station Phase I first power. LPT lead times 128–144 wks; HV substations 3–5 yrs. GE Vernova gas backlog → ~110 GW, sold out through 2030. Uranium LT ~$90/lb (14-yr high). 45X battery credit phase-out begins; FEOC claw-back from Dec 31, 2025.
- **2027:** TMI/Crane restart online (~835 MW, Microsoft 20-yr PPA). CFS SPARC first plasma / Q>1 target "as early as 2027." New cable/transformer plants begin coming online.
- **2028:** Russian uranium deliveries end (2024 ban). Cape Station Phase II. Helion "first fusion plant" target (high skepticism). Duane Arnold restart (Google).
- **2029–2030:** US DC demand 345–490 TWh (up to 655 by 2035 high case). Gas turbine slots essentially full through 2030. First US SMRs (NuScale ~2030; TerraPower Kemmerer ~2030; Kairos/Oklo ~2028–2030). Copper mine output peaks (~24 Mt). Urenco Capenhurst HALEU ~2031.
- **2031–2036:** Interconnection queues (~2 TW) slowly clearing under FERC Order 2023. Bulk of SMR deployments. CFS ARC commercial (~400 MW, early 2030s). Copper deficit toward ~30% by 2035. Fusion "power on grid" national target mid-2030s.

## 2. Value-chain map
Generation (gas turbines GE Vernova/Siemens Energy/Mitsubishi — oligopoly sold out to 2030; nuclear restarts Constellation/Holtec/NextEra; SMRs NuScale/X-energy/Oklo/Kairos/TerraPower; solar+storage; EGS Fervo; fusion CFS/Helion) → Transmission/grid equipment (**LPTs & GSUs** Hitachi Energy/Siemens Energy/GE Vernova/Eaton/ABB; HV switchgear; **HVDC cable** Prysmian/Nexans/NKT — tightest layer) → Distribution (MV switchgear, cables — Eaton/Schneider/ABB) → Behind-the-meter (aeroderivatives, gensets) → Fuels (uranium Cameco/Kazatomprom; conversion/enrichment/HALEU Centrus/Urenco/Orano; gas/LNG) → Labor (**journeyman electricians 45–70% of DC construction cost** — the most under-priced bottleneck).

## 3. Structural scarcities

### A. Large power transformers & GSUs — binds NOW, hardest moat
Lead times 128 wks (LPT) / 144 wks (GSU), some to 4 yrs; ~30% LPT fleet shortfall (Wood Mackenzie); GSU demand +274% since 2019. Sub-bottlenecks: grain-oriented electrical steel + skilled winding labor. Binds 2025–2028; eases ~2027–2029 as new plants (3–4 yr builds) ramp; demand stays elevated. Moat extreme. No technical substitute. Expressions: Hitachi Energy (6501.T), Siemens Energy, GE Vernova, **Eaton (ETN, +$1.5B capacity)**, ABB, Schneider; grain-oriented electrical steel suppliers.

### B. Gas turbine slots — binds NOW through 2030, oligopoly
Lead times stretched from ~2.5–3 yrs to up to 7–8 yrs. GE Vernova ~62 GW backlog + reservations (Q3'25) → ~110 GW by YE2026, **sold out through 2030**; backlog ~$150B (+25% in 2025). Only 3 credible OEMs. Capacity doubling (Mitsubishi; GE Vernova/Siemens) gives partial 2027–2029 relief but backlog stubborn. Moat very high (3-player oligopoly, pricing power rising). Expressions: **GE Vernova (GEV)**, **Siemens Energy (ENR.DE)**, Mitsubishi Heavy (7011.T).

### C. HALEU / Enrichment — binds 2027–2032, geopolitical moat
Only Russia & China produce HALEU at scale; US Russian-uranium ban (deliveries end 2028). Domestic covers only 10–25% of needs; DOE demand ~50 t/yr HALEU by 2035. SMRs need HALEU — **fuel could gate the SMR rollout**. Moat high (security-cleared facilities, multi-year licensing). Expressions: **Centrus (LEU)** — only US-owned HALEU producer (>1 t in 2025; $900M DOE award); Urenco (private); Cameco (CCJ) upstream; Orano (private).

### D. Uranium (upstream) — structural deficit across decade
LT contract ~$90/lb (highest since 2008); Kazatomprom 2026 target −10%; Cameco McArthur below plan; Niger SOMAÏR zero in 2025. Utilities undercontracted 13 straight yrs; Meta signed up to 7.8 GW nuclear (Q1'26); net deficit 2025–2045 ~1.9B lb. Expressions: **Cameco (CCJ)**, Kazatomprom (KAP.L), Sprott Physical (U.UN), NexGen (NXE), Denison (DNN).

### E. Skilled electrical labor — NON-CONSENSUS, binds NOW
Electrical = 45–70% of DC construction cost (IBEW). Microsoft's Brad Smith (Mar 2026) called the electrician shortage the "#1 problem" for US DC expansion. ~300k+ electricians needed; ~20k retiring/yr; journeyman pay ~$120k–$200k. Apprenticeship pipelines take 4–5 yrs → relief late-decade at earliest. Human-capital, geographically concentrated, unionized; can't be imported quickly. Expressions: EPC/electrical contractors — **Quanta Services (PWR)**, **MYR Group (MYRG)**, **Comfort Systems (FIX)**, **EMCOR (EME)**, **MasTec (MTZ)**, **Sterling (STRL)**.

### F. HVDC & HV cable — binds 2025–2029
DC cable waitlists >5 yrs; new plants 3–4 yrs; Prysmian transmission backlog ~€16B; Nexans ~€8.1B. Eases ~2027–2030 as capacity + cable-laying vessels ramp. Moat very high (few makers, vessel-constrained). Expressions: **Prysmian (PRY.MI)**, **Nexans (NEX.PA)**, NKT (NKT.CO).

### G. Switchgear (MV/HV) — binds 2025–2027, easing fastest
HV substations 3–5 yrs; MV tight. Eaton +$1.5B, Schneider +$140M. Lead times targeted toward ~24 wks by 2027 — relief earlier than transformers/turbines. Expressions: **Eaton (ETN)**, **Schneider (SU.PA)**, ABB, Hitachi Energy.

### H. Copper — binds late-decade into 2030s, slowest moat
IEA: 30% supply deficit by 2035; mine output peaks late-2020s (~24 Mt) → <19 Mt by 2035; 17-yr avg development; grades −40% since 1991; DC alone ~250–550 kt/yr by 2030. Substitution (aluminum at margin, recycling) partial. Expressions: Freeport (FCX), Southern Copper (SCCO), Ivanhoe, Teck; copper royalties.

### I. Interconnection queue / grid access — NON-CONSENSUS soft bottleneck
~2 TW (1.6× US grid) stuck; waits 5+ yrs; CAISO/PJM paused applications. FERC Order 2023 helps but takes years. Drives behind-the-meter gas (bridging the grid entirely). Expression: Bloom Energy (BE), Cummins (CMI), Generac (GNRC).

## 4. Non-consensus (under-priced)
1. **Skilled electricians** ("#1 problem" yet barely investable directly → PWR/MYRG/FIX/EME); most durable (human capital can't be ordered with lead time). 2. **Large power transformers/GSUs** (true single point of failure, longest least-substitutable lead time, ~30% shortfall). 3. **HALEU enrichment** (could gate the entire SMR thesis; Centrus the near-pure-play). 4. **HVDC cable + cable-laying vessels** (>5-yr waitlists, vessel-constrained). 5. **Interconnection access** as the driver of behind-the-meter gas.

## 5. Confidence & the bear case
High confidence (binds 2026–2029): transformers, gas turbine slots, HVDC cable, skilled labor, uranium tightness (physically observable, slow supply response). Medium: HALEU timing (depends on SMR cadence); copper deficit magnitude. Lower/asymmetric: SMR first deployments (2030 targets slip); fusion (Helion 2028 and CFS net-energy-2027 aggressive — optionality not base case; DOE targets grid power "mid-2030s").
**Bear case respected:** 30–50% of planned 2026 US DC builds may be delayed/canceled; Nadella concedes "there will be an overbuild"; AI token costs fell ~280× in 2 yrs; each GPU generation 2–3× more efficient/watt; DeepSeek scare showed forecast fragility. **However** Jevons + reasoning-model token blow-up keep absolute demand rising, and the bottleneck is *cumulative under-investment in manufacturing capacity*, not just AI. Even a 30% demand haircut leaves equipment supply chains tight (grid-modernization + electrification + reshoring). **Asymmetry favors equipment/fuel/labor chokepoints over generation-tech bets.**
