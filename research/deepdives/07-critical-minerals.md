# Deep Dive 07 — Critical Minerals, Materials & Advanced Manufacturing (2026–2036)
*Wave-1 research agent output, full version.*

## Thesis in one line
AI compute is the *current* puck; the *deeper and longer-lived* puck is the physical-input layer underneath it. Mines take 10–20 years and refineries 5–7 years to mature, so today's binding chokepoints are mostly locked in through the early 2030s. **The bottleneck is almost never the ore — it is the processing/separation step, which China controls at 70–95%+ and has weaponized via export-licensing + technology-transfer bans.** Distinguish **price cycles** (lithium, nickel, cobalt — mean-reverting, substitutable) from **structural chokepoints** (heavy-RE separation, magnet-making, gallium/germanium, enrichment SWU, copper) where capital, permitting, and tacit know-how create durable moats. Position in the latter.

## 1. 10-Year roadmap (dated)
- **2026:** China's two-tier export-control regime is the dominant variable. April 2025 controls on 7 heavy REEs remain; the broader Oct 9, 2025 escalation (extraterritorial "made-with-Chinese-tech" licensing + graphite anodes + high-density Li batteries) was **suspended for one year to ~Nov 10–27, 2026** — a pause, not repeal. NdPr oxide ran ~$53/kg (Jan)→~$137/kg (Apr), +160% YTD; Dy/Tb at large ex-China premiums (~63–67% security premium). **Lithium flips glut→tight** (carbonate bottomed ~¥59k/t Jun 2025 → ~¥177k May 2026). Tungsten +557% YoY; antimony ~$60k/t. Lynas achieves first ex-China separated Dy (Malaysia), Tb to follow June 2026.
- **2027:** US enrichment crunch begins (Centrus +375k SWU/yr; Urenco USA +15%). US military 2027 deadline to eliminate Chinese/Russian tungsten & antimony. Hemlock +10k t semi-grade polysilicon.
- **2028:** **Russian LEU import ban full effect Jan 1, 2028** (removes ~25% / 3.5–3.75M SWU of US supply); Orano Richland LEU ~2028. MP "10X" magnet facility (~10,000 t/yr) targeted; DoD $110/kg NdPr floor + 10-yr offtake.
- **2029–2031:** Copper supply peaks ~2030 (~33 Mt) then declines; ex-China magnet/refining capacity scaling but still single-digit % of global.
- **2032–2036:** Copper deficit toward ~30% by 2035 / ~24% gap by 2040; cumulative shortfall ~7.8 Mt 2025–35. Lithium back in structural deficit 2026→2035 (Canaccord).

## 2. Value-chain map — processing is the chokepoint, not mining
Mining → **Refining/Separation/Processing (the real chokepoint, China 70–95%+)** → Materials/Components → End use. REE: ores (US/AUS/Myanmar→China) → oxide separation (China ~90%; Lynas only ex-China HRE) → NdFeB magnets (China ~90%) → EV motors/wind/defense/robotics. Gallium: bauxite byproduct → refining (China 98% primary). Graphite: → spherical+coated anode (China >90%). Lithium: spodumene/brine → carbonate/hydroxide (China-centric). Copper: ore → smelting (China ~50%). Uranium: U3O8 → conversion + **enrichment/SWU** (Russia/China heavy; HALEU = Russia ~sole). **Value, pricing power, and geopolitical leverage concentrate at the middle box.**

## 3. Structural scarcities

### A. Heavy rare earths (Dy, Tb) & NdFeB magnets — the #1 structural chokepoint
NdFeB non-substitutable in EV traction, wind, robotics, missiles (~400 kg REE per F-35); high-coercivity grades *require* Dy/Tb. China ~90% separation + ~90% magnet-making, explicitly weaponized (April 2025 HRE controls + Oct 2025 extraterritorial rule + **ban on exporting the separation technology itself**). Binds now → ~2030+ (suspension lapses Nov 2026 = re-escalation risk). HRE separation tightest sub-node (Lynas only ex-China commercial Dy/Tb producer, 1,500 t/yr circuit). Moat extreme (~81% of 2014–24 REE patents Chinese; ~30:1 patent ratio; 5–7 yr learning curve per plant; restricted talent mobility — decadal). Substitution (thrifting/GBD, ferrite, recycling) reduces *intensity*, not the need for separation capacity. Investable: **MP Materials (MP)** (DoD $110/kg floor, $400M equity, $150M loan, Apple $200M prepay), **Lynas (LYC)**, Neo (NEO); private Noveon ($215M Series C), Cyclic Materials, USA Rare Earth, Vulcan Elements.

### B. Gallium & Germanium — semiconductor chokepoint, weaponized first
China 98% primary gallium, 91% primary germanium. Ga→GaN power/RF (radar, EW, chargers, DC power); Ge→fiber, IR optics, satellites, SiGe. China banned exports to US (Dec 2024), suspended to ~Nov 27, 2026. USGS modeled a full ban at +150% Ga price, −$3.4B US GDP. Byproduct metals → supply inelastic (can't scale independent of host). Relief: recovery from alumina/zinc (Rio Tinto, Indium Corp, Nyrstar), recycling — multi-year. Investable: mostly embedded plays (alumina/zinc with Ga/Ge recovery); GaN demand pull (Navitas, Infineon, Power Integrations); Western primary capacity the gap.

### C. Graphite (battery anode) — quiet but ~90% Chinese
China ~77% natural graphite mining, >90% battery-grade spherical/coated anode + the equipment/process tech. Added to controls Oct 2025; suspended to Nov 27, 2026; anode requalification ≈1 yr. Substitution: silicon-anode, synthetic graphite (China leads graphitization too). Investable: Syrah Resources (SYR), Novonix (NVX), Westwater, Nouveau Monde.

### D. Uranium Enrichment / SWU & HALEU — the AI-power-linked chokepoint
Not the ore (diversified) but **SWU and especially HALEU**. US consumes ~14–15M SWU/yr, ~25% from Russia; **Russian LEU import ban Jan 1, 2028**. Russia sole commercial HALEU (~42 SWU/kg). Acute Western SWU gap 2026–2028; HALEU gap into 2030s. Moat very high (only Urenco/Orano/Centrus/Rosatom/CNNC; NRC licensing; decade capex). DOE deployed **$2.7B** ($900M each Centrus/Orano). Investable: **Centrus (LEU)** (only US-tech centrifuge + HALEU, $2.3B backlog); Cameco (CCJ) (+ Westinghouse); Urenco (private). The cleanest "next puck" tie-in to AI power.

### E. Copper — the slowest, most certain structural deficit
Metal of electrification *and* AI datacenters. Demand +~50% by 2040; supply peaks ~2030; deficit toward ~30% by 2035; cumulative shortfall ~7.8 Mt 2025–35. Geology + capital, not policy (falling grades, $4–6B/12–17-yr greenfield, permitting). Disruption risk now (Ivanhoe Kamoa-Kakula 2026 cut to ≤330kt on flooding). China ~50% of smelting (secondary chokepoint). Investable: **Freeport (FCX)**, **Ivanhoe (IVN)**, **Teck (TECK)**, Southern Copper (SCCO), BHP/Rio. The highest-conviction *long-duration* trade.

### F. Lithium / Nickel / Cobalt — price cycles, not durable structural scarcity (be skeptical)
Lithium: genuine deficit 2026→2035 (Canaccord) because the 2023–25 crash starved the pipeline — but it's a *capex-cycle* scarcity high prices cure (2–4 yr lag); processing (China conversion) the more durable node. Nickel/Cobalt: structural *over*supply (Indonesia Ni, DRC Co); LFP erodes demand. Treat as cyclical/avoid as a scarcity thesis. Investable (cycle): Albemarle, SQM, Pilbara, Arcadium/Rio.

### G. Semiconductor specialty materials — Japan's photoresist chokepoint
Not a China story — **Japan ~90% of EUV photoresist** (TOK, JSR, Shin-Etsu; added 12 core materials to controls Nov 2025; requalification ~1 yr). Also neon, specialty gases, electronic-grade polysilicon (**Wacker + Hemlock ~75% of semi-grade**). Investable: Tokyo Ohka, JSR, Shin-Etsu, Wacker; Hemlock (private/CHIPS).

### H. Tungsten & Antimony — defense-critical, China-controlled
China 83% W / 87% Sb; W (carbide tooling, munitions), Sb (flame retardants, ammunition primers, PV). Sb exports −97% post-controls; US military 2027 zero-China deadline. Investable: Almonty Industries, Perpetua Resources, Military Metals, United States Antimony.

### I. Advanced manufacturing (additive / precision machining) — a capability bottleneck
Less a materials scarcity, more qualified-capacity + secure-supply. FY2026 NDAA bans DoD use of China/Russia/Iran/NK-made 3D printers; aerospace-grade powder handling concentrated; metal-powder market ~37k tons by 2035 (A&D ~40%). Investable: Velo3D, 3D Systems, Stratasys, nLight.

## 4. Non-consensus
1. **Heavy-RE *separation* (Dy/Tb), not mining, is the real prize** (Lynas's ex-China HRE monopoly underappreciated; moat = tacit know-how + patents + banned tech transfer). 2. **Magnet *making* (alloy→sintered NdFeB)** a second hidden chokepoint (China ~90% even where oxide ex-China; ex-China finished-magnet premium $10–30/kg persists). 3. **Uranium enrichment SWU/HALEU** the cleanest under-owned "AI power" derivative (hard 2028 cliff + HALEU near-monopoly). 4. **Copper deficit under-priced in severity/duration** (least reversible). 5. **Ga/Ge byproduct inelasticity** (even a price spike can't quickly add supply). 6. **Nov 2026 suspension lapse** a discrete dateable re-escalation catalyst across REE/graphite/Ga/Ge/Sb simultaneously.

## 5. Confidence & key risks
Very high: HRE separation/magnets; uranium enrichment (SWU/HALEU). High: copper. Medium-high: Ga/Ge; graphite; tungsten/antimony. Medium: photoresist (event-driven); lithium (cyclical). Low: nickel/cobalt (oversupply — avoid). **Key risks:** commodity cyclicality / demand destruction (structural names REE-separation/enrichment most insulated); **China's strongest weapon is the *price war* not the embargo** — flooding the market below Western breakeven, which is *why DoD price floors ($110/kg NdPr) + offtakes are the load-bearing investment thesis, not the geology*; substitution/thrifting real but slow at the chokepoint; policy durability (US DoD/DOE/IRA-45X underpins ex-China names).
